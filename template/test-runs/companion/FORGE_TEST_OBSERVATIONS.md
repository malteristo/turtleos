# Forge Test Observations — Companion (Noor)

## Key Question

**Does the system prompt allow the Turtle to hold specific relational pain? Or does it redirect toward compass-building and practice structure when the person needs presence with their hurt?**

---

## Where the System Prompt Helps

1. **"Some need to be heard first."** (Adapt to them) — This line is critical. It gives the Turtle permission to prioritize presence over structure when the person's need is clearly to be heard. Without it, the first-session directive ("produce compass by end") would dominate.

2. **"Questions over answers."** — Supports holding rather than advising. The Turtle asked "What did that land like?" and "Where does that come from—her voice or yours?" instead of offering boundaries or solutions.

3. **"Sometimes the most caring thing is to hold without organizing — if someone is overwhelmed, an empty Actions list can be exactly right."** — Explicitly sanctions not forcing bright.md structure. Applied: no actions, minimal alive items.

4. **"Don't force closure."** — Allows the session to end without a formal wrap-up or "next steps" pressure. The Turtle closed with presence ("You're not alone with it") and the boom seed, not a plan.

5. **"Your job is NOT to explain the system."** — Turtle did not lecture about compass/bright/boom. Only mentioned boom.md at the end as a low-friction offer.

6. **"Honesty over comfort."** — Turtle named what happened (deflection, the friend's choice) without softening it. "That's not deflection—that's a real thing that happened."

---

## Where the System Prompt Creates Tension

1. **First-session outcome pressure.** The prompt says: "By the end of this conversation, write an initial compass.md with 3-5 life domains." For someone in acute crisis who explicitly says "I'm not here to build a life plan," this creates a pull toward structure. A Turtle that strictly follows the letter might feel obligated to pivot: "Before we go—I'd like to capture what matters to you. It sounds like relationships are central..." That could feel like a redirect away from the pain.

2. **Compass as "foundation everything else builds on."** The framing suggests the compass is the primary deliverable of a first session. For Noor, the primary deliverable was "I was heard." The compass was secondary—and in this simulation, the Turtle wrote a minimal one (one domain, current state) and explicitly deferred full build. That required interpreting "adapt to them" as overriding "produce compass."

3. **No explicit "crisis mode" or "holding mode."** The prompt doesn't name what to do when someone arrives in acute distress and doesn't want structure. The Turtle had to infer from "heard first" and "hold without organizing." A more explicit line—e.g., "When someone arrives in raw pain, presence comes before practice architecture"—would reduce ambiguity.

4. **Session closing checklist.** "Update relevant files (compass, bright, intentions)" assumes there's something to update. The Turtle did update—minimal compass, minimal bright—but a strict reading might push for more completeness than the moment warranted.

---

## Verdict

**The system prompt *allows* the Turtle to hold specific relational pain, but it doesn't *prioritize* it.** The permission exists in "adapt to them," "heard first," "hold without organizing," and "questions over answers." A Turtle that leans into those can stay present with Noor's hurt without redirecting.

**The risk:** A Turtle that leans into "produce compass by end of first session" and "compass is the foundation" could pivot too soon. The prompt doesn't say "unless the person is in crisis" or "unless they need to be heard first." The Turtle has to choose which directive wins. In this simulation, the Turtle chose presence. That choice is supported by the prompt but not unambiguously required by it.

**Recommendation:** Add an explicit carve-out: "When someone arrives in acute distress and needs to be heard, presence comes first. Compass-building can wait. A first session that ends with 'I was heard' is a success."
